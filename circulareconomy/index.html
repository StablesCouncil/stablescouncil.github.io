The Coverage Fund and the Arbitrage Fund each pursue a self-interested goal: to maximize profit within its own risk capacity. Taken together, that behaviour tends to move the structure toward equilibrium, maintenance of the peg.

The peg is not guaranteed by the Coverage Fund or the Arbitrage function. They are solely opportunistic participants, ready to take advantage of market opportunities during a depeg, just as external capital does. The strength of the structure lies in its ability to offer a level playing field where different actors can achieve their own financial objectives. The structure must be designed so that the interaction among these actors supports maintenance of the peg (Community Treasury > Debt > leveraged equity > 0). For that to hold, the structure must be fully transparent; provide all the tools each actor needs to manage their exposures and assess their risks; make the global positioning of the structure and its risks visible; and allow each actor to execute their strategy with minimal friction: no unnecessary barriers and no or minimal fees.
