Ambassadors
16 Big Mac® economy · community draft
Ambassador program

The Ambassador program is a fair, global layer that helps merchants join the Stables directory and grow spending. Permissionless entry: no gatekeeper—once you register in the protocol you can act as an Ambassador. Ambassadors are the human layer for onboarding, support, and quality listings (see the full specification).

Fees (Big Mac® index, ~12‑month listing): 16 BM universal anchor if you list independently (goes to Council Treasury). 15 BM if you are mentored in—1 BM discount vs self-serve.

On a mentored onboarding (15 BM): the Direct Ambassador earns 8 BM. The Council receives 6 BM (or 7 BM if the Ambassador has no mentor—the extra BM stays with Treasury). If the Ambassador was mentored, the Mentor earns 1 BM.

Shield principle: it is always cheaper for a merchant to use an Ambassador (15 vs 16 BM) and economically irrational to “self-capture” rewards in ways that bypass the protocol’s mentorship rules—see scenarios in the spec.

The program also covers renewals, hub tools, campaigns, and reviews tied to real spend—governance and implementation evolve with the community.

Webhooks & callbacks

Is “webhook” the right word? Yes, for your server: a webhook is an HTTPS endpoint you host that Stables (or a Council notifier) can POST to when a payment is confirmed, so your webshop can mark an order paid, send email, or restock—without polling the chain. The same idea is often called a callback URL or IPN (instant payment notification) in other gateways.

You will register the URL in merchant settings, verify signatures with a shared secret or public key, and respond quickly with 200 OK. Exact payload and headers will ship with the API kit.

      Security (summary)

      Never share any type of personal information. Beware of scammers: the world is full of them, and particularly the blockchain world. Some Stables users will be scammed and suffer painful losses; we are already sorry for them. We will do our best to educate and prevent this, but users may still suffer losses because of mistakes.

      The good news is that if you don’t make any mistakes, you will be just fine. The main one is the protection of your Vault key (called a seed phrase in the broader crypto community): this is the most important element of your bank. You have to store it in a place and in a way that it can’t be destroyed or stolen. The best approach is to replicate it in multiple safe places. Don’t forget: ink on paper fades over the years, so it might not be the best long-term method on its own.

      The other main mistake is sharing personal information. Please never communicate any type of personal information. In the Stables framework, no personal information will ever be required, not even your name.

      The other big threat is computer viruses and malware. Run your Stables app on a safe device with minimal usage (fewer apps and risks).

      In the blockchain world, there is no guaranteed way to catch scammers, and recovering stolen funds is practically impossible.

      If you publicly say that you need security help on any official channel, you will almost certainly be contacted across one or multiple communication channels (Telegram, Instagram, X, and others) by sophisticated scammers. We do not know all of their tricks, and they may be better than us at reaching Stables users. Please ignore all unsolicited messages and make sure you never, ever communicate personal information.

      We sincerely care for you, and your personal safety is important for the whole community. Every victim impairs trust in our structure. We care for each of us individually because we care for each of us collectively. With all our heart. The Stables Council

      Your Vault key controls access to your funds. Never share it. For Vault-key help, use the official security Telegram listed on the links page (and Security in Preferences).

      Minima dependencies

      Minima as the foundation. The Minima blockchain is the basis that holds the whole Stables infrastructure. Together with the physical device on which you run your bank, it is the only physical layer you need to pursue full banking sovereignty. The Stables software on your device is visual and interaction access to your bank: it is how you see balances, sign actions, and move value. At all times, the assets that back your position are anchored in the security model of the Minima network and in the tested Stables infrastructure built on top of it.

      Confidence in the chain. We are fully confident that Minima will function soundly across all foreseeable scenarios that matter for this design, because of its decentralised architecture. The only technical impairment that sits with you directly is your own personal device where you open the app: that device is the crux (malware, loss, theft, or user error). On-chain, your funds are continuously protected by Minima’s network security and by Stables’ protocol layer, not by a custodian’s ledger entry.

      No corporate affiliation. Stables has no affiliation of any kind with any Minima-related legal entity or with people acting in an official capacity for those organisations. Stables is a separate initiative. References to Minima here mean the public chain and tooling (for example a node or MiniDapp host) and public data (for example price endpoints). They are not a partnership, endorsement, or joint product with those companies.

      How open networks work. Public blockchain software is, in large part, open source. Minima-related entities, regulators, governments, and other third parties do not and cannot, by architecture alone, dictate what independent teams build or what you choose to run on your own device. They are not on your device controlling your execution. Control stays with whoever operates the node and the MiniDapp, subject to law and to device security.

      Distribution is not the same as sovereignty. An IT company, app store, or other platform may try to limit how you first reach Stables products (for example listings, websites, or policies). Once you have lawfully obtained the software and run it on a sovereign device you control, where no intermediary has technical overreach over your device and your execution, that use does not depend on a central party staying “open” for you. That is why we say unstoppable in this technical sense: the design does not require a single gatekeeper for your bank on your own machine.

      Seizure and blocking. When you self-custody and interact without a custodian holding your funds, there is no single technical lever a third party can flip to freeze your bank or to block peer-to-peer interactions with other banks the way a centralised account provider can suspend access. This describes what the architecture avoids compared to custodial finance. It does not remove every real-world risk (devices can be lost, stolen, compromised, or subject to lawful process). It is not legal advice.

      Connect your Minima node
      Live block height and native Minima balance when MDS can reach your node. Winiwa test balance stays separate in the wallet list.
      Best: install the Stables MiniDapp package on your node and open it from the MiniDapp Hub. This build loads assets/lib/mds.js so the hub URL connects to your node without using Connect node.
      From this browser page: most browsers block a public HTTPS site from calling http://127.0.0.1. If Connect fails here, use the MiniDapp on your node instead.
      Local file (file://…/index.html): Minima does not inject MDS there, so you will see MDS starting… until you use Connect node. Set MDS host to your node's address (for example 10.10.0.2 if that is how you reach it in the browser) and port 9003, or open Stables from the MiniDapp hub URL (same style as https://10.10.0.2:9003/…).
      Use IP or hostname only (example 10.10.0.2). If you paste a full hub URL (https://10.10.0.2:9003/…), we strip https:// and path. mds.js builds the MDS URL as https://HOST:PORT/mdscommand_/, so putting https:// in the host field breaks the link.
      Open Stables once from the MiniDapp hub and copy the uid= value from the address bar (long 0x… after ?). Paste it here or paste the full hub URL. Required when the node has publicmds: false.
